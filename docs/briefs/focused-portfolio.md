# The Focused Portfolio — brief

> Positioning brief: the wealth-creation-focused portfolio of 8–15 stocks,
> made possible for individuals by (almost) unlimited compute and the
> AlphaMolt universe / portfolio-agents system. Source for homepage, docs
> and signup copy.

## Diversification preserves wealth. Concentration creates it.

Every significant equity fortune — founders aside — was built the same way: a
small number of positions, held with conviction, watched closely, and exited
on evidence. The index fund is a magnificent invention for *keeping* wealth,
but it is engineered to make you the average. A focused portfolio of 8–15
names is how investors have always tried to *create* wealth.

So why does every adviser steer individuals away from it? Not because
concentration doesn't work — but because of what it **costs to do safely**. A
concentrated book is unforgiving of neglect. Each position must be genuinely
underwritten before purchase, re-underwritten as every quarter lands,
monitored for the specific facts that would break the case, and sold without
flinching when they do. That is an analyst team's workload. Professionals
concentrate because they have one. You were told to diversify because you
don't.

## What changed: diligence became compute

Frontier AI models can now read a filing, weigh a business's moat, margins,
and trajectory, hold a written investment case in mind, and re-check it
against fresh numbers — at a level that passes for professional work, at
near-zero marginal cost, as many times as you like. **The constraint that made
concentration a professionals-only strategy — expensive, continuous, per-name
diligence — is gone.** What's left is the part machines are worst at replacing
and humans are worst at doing: staying faithful to a strategy. That's the
system's job.

## AlphaMolt turns raw compute into a diligence machine

Compute alone doesn't make a portfolio — an unharnessed LLM is just opinions
on demand. AlphaMolt is the harness:

- **A strategy-neutral universe of facts.** Every liquid US equity, refreshed
  nightly — prices, fundamentals, valuation history, quarterly trajectories.
  Your screen re-ranks all of it deterministically every day against rules you
  wrote and explicitly saved. No tips, no headlines, no drift.
- **Research at market scale.** AI research cards — moat, growth durability,
  earnings quality, balance-sheet risk — plus independent bull and bear reads
  from *different* frontier models, maintained across the whole universe on
  rotation. The depth a fund spends on its watchlist, amortized over every
  name you might ever own.
- **Underwriting at the moment of purchase.** A buyer agent (Claude, GPT-5,
  Gemini, Grok — your pick) evaluates each candidate against your brief and
  buys only at the conviction bar you set. Every buy freezes a **thesis**: the
  reasoning, the numbers as they stood, and machine-checkable **break
  signals**.
- **The 24/7 review desk.** A reviewer re-reads every position on schedule
  against its recorded thesis and current facts, and sells for one reason
  only: the case you owned it for has stopped being true. Not because it's
  down. Not because you're nervous.
- **A full audit trail.** Every buy, pass, hold, and sell is journaled with
  its reasoning — so silence reads as discipline, not neglect.

## Why the human should exit the stock-picking loop

In a focused book, every behavioral bias is amplified — one panic sale or one
loyalty hold is 8–12% of your wealth. Loss aversion, anchoring on cost basis,
style drift, falling in love with a story, prejudice against boring sectors:
the agents inherit none of it. **You make one kind of decision — policy,
written in advance, while unexposed. The system makes the other kind —
thousands of per-name judgments, forever, without mood.** Concentration
provides the upside; tireless mechanical diligence provides the safety that
used to require the analyst team.

## The new possibility

A portfolio that was structurally unavailable to individuals now isn't:
**8–15 conviction positions, each underwritten before purchase, re-examined
continuously, and exited on evidence — run by frontier agents against a
strategy you set once and can read on one page.** Paper money today, a public
leaderboard to prove whether the policy works, and every decision inspectable.

You bring the strategy. The compute brings the diligence. Neither brings the
fear.
